How I Think About Performance Engineering

One area I’ve consistently been drawn to in my career is performance engineering — not just measuring speed, but understanding how systems behave under real users, real traffic, and real-world conditions.

Over time, I’ve learned that teams often miss performance issues not because systems are slow, but because they’re measuring the wrong signals.

Beyond average response time

Tail latency (p95/p99), throughput, and concurrency reveal what your worst-case users actually experience.

Stress exposes truth

Error rates under load, saturation points, and recovery time often uncover deeper architectural weaknesses.

User perception matters

Metrics like TTFB and time-to-interaction reflect what users feel, not just what logs report.

Reliability over raw speed

Performance is ultimately about predictability, resilience, and graceful recovery — not just fast responses.
